"I asked three AI models the same questions.
One was brilliant, one was confused, and one confidently lied".

As part of an experiment, I decided to compare three large language models:

GPT-4

Claude

Gemini

The idea was simple: ask them the same questions and see who performed best.

The reality? It felt like hosting a panel discussion where one expert was brilliant, one was philosophical, and one occasionally hallucinated with great confidence.

The first test was a coding problem: write a Python function for data preprocessing.

GPT-4 produced a clean solution with good comments.

Claude wrote a longer explanation and even added edge-case handling.

Gemini… also produced code, but included a variable that mysteriously appeared from another dimension.

Next came reasoning tasks.

Claude impressed me with its structured answers and logical breakdowns. It felt like reading a well-organized technical blog.

GPT-4 balanced clarity and accuracy, making it excellent for practical problem solving.

Gemini was fast and sometimes creative, but occasionally misunderstood the question in spectacular ways.

The funniest test was asking all three models to explain a complex concept “like I’m five.”

Claude wrote a story.
GPT-4 gave a friendly analogy.
Gemini created something that felt halfway between a children’s book and a philosophical debate.

So which model won?

Honestly, none of them.

Each model had strengths depending on the task.

The real takeaway was this: choosing the right model is less about hype and more about matching the tool to the problem.

And sometimes… testing them just makes for great entertainment.
